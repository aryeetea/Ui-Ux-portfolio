01 Dear Stranger
Full Stack
2024
+

An anonymous letter-writing product designed to make emotional honesty feel safe, intimate, and unexpectedly beautiful.

The goal was not just to build a functional social platform. It was to design a digital atmosphere where strangers would trust the interface enough to say something real. That meant every product decision, from the first screen to the send flow, had to reduce emotional friction.

North Star Make vulnerability feel safe
Core Flow Discover, write, send, reflect
Key Constraint High emotion, low overwhelm

The Problem

Most anonymous platforms feel unsafe because anonymity is usually paired with chaos. I needed to reverse that pattern and create a product where emotional distance felt calming instead of risky.

My Approach

I built the visual system around quiet contrast: deep space backgrounds, restrained gold accents, soft motion, and deliberate pacing. The product language avoids pressure and makes the act of writing feel ceremonial rather than performative.

Interaction Design

The star map was designed as both discovery mechanism and emotional metaphor. Real users become constellations, which turns browsing into a reflective experience instead of a crowded feed. Motion was tuned to feel alive but never noisy.

AI Decision

The OpenAI-assisted writing companion does not take over authorship. It prompts reflection, helps users continue when they feel stuck, and protects the personal quality of the letter instead of flattening it into generic text.

Outcome

The final product feels emotionally distinct from traditional social apps. It combines product design, frontend craft, and systems thinking into an experience where the atmosphere is part of the usability.

02 DAS Sterile Training
Client Work
2024
+

A healthcare training site redesigned around clarity, trust, and client autonomy instead of visual clutter.

This project served a real business with real operational pressure: prospective students needed to quickly understand offerings, trust the organization, and take action, while the client needed a site they could confidently maintain after handoff.

North Star Trust at first glance
Core Flow Learn, verify, enroll
Key Constraint Easy client updates after launch

The Problem

Healthcare audiences do not reward visual experimentation that creates doubt. The challenge was to make the brand feel credible and polished while still giving the client a modern web presence that did not feel generic.

Information Architecture

I prioritized quick wayfinding: course information, certification credibility, and contact paths needed to surface early. The structure was designed so hiring managers and students could each reach their goals without scanning unnecessary content.

Design System

The UI direction leans clean and disciplined: strong type hierarchy, generous white space, accessible contrast, and components that reinforce professionalism rather than decoration. The visual confidence comes from restraint.

CMS Strategy

I paired the frontend with WordPress intentionally so the client could manage updates independently. The design had to be flexible enough for edited content without breaking the visual rhythm or layout quality.

Outcome

The finished site supports both perception and operations: it feels established to visitors and practical to the client, which is the kind of product thinking that makes a site useful after launch, not just during presentation.

03 Gobɛ Hemaa
Full Stack
2024
+

A culturally rooted food-ordering experience designed to feel warm, trustworthy, and operationally reliable under real payment conditions.

This was more than a restaurant landing page. It needed to support active ordering, multiple payment behaviors, and the emotional layer of representing Ghanaian food culture with pride instead of flattening it into a generic delivery UI.

North Star Celebrate culture without sacrificing usability
Core Flow Browse, customize, pay, confirm
Key Constraint Real transactions across multiple gateways

The Problem

Food ordering products often default to bland sameness. Here, the challenge was to design something that felt unmistakably Ghanaian while still being legible, fast, and familiar enough for customers to trust with payment.

Brand Translation

I used warmth, rich color, and celebratory language to express the personality of the brand, but kept the interface structure disciplined. Cultural expression lives in tone, palette, and product storytelling, not in clutter.

Checkout Complexity

The core systems challenge was payment resilience. I integrated Paystack, Flutterwave, and Hubtel so customers had options across different banking and mobile money contexts, each with its own success and failure states.

Backend Design

The data model had to protect order integrity. Orders, payments, and line items were coordinated through transactions so partial failures would not create ghost purchases or ambiguous payment records.

Outcome

The final product balances identity and infrastructure. It feels specific to the business it serves, while still handling the serious, less visible work of checkout trust, state management, and payment reliability.
