Case Studies

How I think through product, interface, and implementation.

These are fuller case studies focused on the challenge, the design decisions, and what made the final experience work.

The product needed to feel intimate without becoming chaotic.

Dear Stranger was built around a simple question: how do you make anonymity feel safe instead of unpredictable? Most anonymous platforms create distance, but they rarely create trust. I wanted this product to feel calm, emotionally intelligent, and intentionally designed for honesty.

I approached the interface like an atmosphere, not just a set of screens. The visual language leaned into darkness, soft gold accents, and quiet motion so the product felt reflective rather than loud. Every part of the experience was meant to reduce emotional friction and help users feel comfortable saying something real.

The Problem

Anonymous social spaces often feel unsafe because the experience is built around noise, not care. The challenge was to create emotional openness without making the product feel exposed or chaotic.

Design Approach

I focused on slow pacing, clear hierarchy, and restrained interaction design. Instead of crowding the user with activity, the interface creates space for reflection and intentional writing.

Interaction Thinking

The star map discovery layer turned real users into constellations. It made discovery feel poetic and exploratory, while still serving a clear functional role in helping people find letters and connections.

Outcome

The final product feels emotionally distinct from typical social apps. It combines frontend craft, product thinking, and atmosphere into an experience where usability and feeling support each other.

This site had to feel credible immediately.

This project served a real healthcare training business, which meant the site had to do more than look polished. It needed to communicate trust quickly, make information easy to navigate, and support real business goals without overwhelming the user.

I treated credibility as a design requirement. The structure emphasized course information, certification details, and clear contact paths, while the UI stayed disciplined and readable. On the implementation side, I paired the frontend with WordPress so the client could manage updates confidently after launch.

The Problem

Healthcare audiences are sensitive to clutter and ambiguity. The site needed to feel credible, accessible, and clear from the first interaction.

Information Architecture

I prioritized quick wayfinding so visitors could understand the training offerings, verify the organization, and take the next step without unnecessary searching.

Design System

The interface used strong type hierarchy, clean spacing, and simple components to create confidence through clarity rather than decoration.

Outcome

The final site supports both visitor trust and client autonomy. It feels professional to users and practical for the business maintaining it.

The visual language needed cultural warmth without sacrificing checkout clarity.

Gobɛ Hemaa was about more than ordering food online. It was an opportunity to design a product that felt culturally rooted and visually warm while still handling the serious work of e-commerce, state management, and payment reliability.

I used expressive color, familiar ordering patterns, and product storytelling to make the experience feel inviting. Underneath that, the architecture had to stay disciplined so customers could move through browsing, customization, payment, and confirmation without confusion.

The Problem

Food ordering products often become visually generic. This needed to feel specific to the brand and culture it represented without sacrificing usability.

Brand Translation

I used warmth, color, and tone to express personality, while keeping the layout structured enough to support fast browsing and easy decision-making.

Checkout Complexity

The payment experience had to work across Paystack, Flutterwave, and Hubtel, which meant designing for multiple flows, success states, and failure states.

Outcome

The final product feels vibrant and specific to the business it serves while remaining reliable in the places where trust matters most.
