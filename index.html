01 Dear Stranger
Full Stack
2024
+

Dear Stranger is a web app I designed and built entirely from scratch — a space for anonymous, heartfelt letters between strangers. The core product challenge was making vulnerability feel safe. The dark space aesthetic wasn't decorative; it created emotional distance that actually lowered the barrier to writing honestly.

The interactive star map was the hardest piece: each floating hub represents a real user, generated from their profile data and positioned in a 2D space using a weighted clustering algorithm. Framer Motion handled the smooth orbital animations. The entire thing had to feel alive without feeling chaotic.

OpenAI integration powers a gentle writing companion — not rewriting letters, but asking the right questions to help users find their own words.

02 DAS Sterile Training
Client Work
2024
+

A real client engagement for a healthcare sterile processing training company. The brief was clear: professional, trustworthy, easy to navigate. Healthcare hiring managers needed to quickly verify credentials and find course information — every second of friction costs a potential student.

I led the design and frontend implementation using React and TypeScript, with Tailwind CSS for a clean, consistent UI. The WordPress CMS integration was intentional — the client needed to update content themselves without touching code. I designed a custom WordPress theme that matched the React frontend exactly.

Accessibility was a priority throughout: clear typographic hierarchy, high contrast text, keyboard-navigable course catalog, and mobile-first responsive layouts for students accessing on phone.

03 Gobɛ Hemaa
Full Stack
2024
+

Built for my cousin's Ghanaian food business — a full stack food ordering platform that handles real money and real orders. "Gobɛ" is a beloved Ghanaian dish, and "Hemaa" means queen. The design needed to feel warm, celebratory, and deeply Ghanaian without being a caricature.

The payment integration was the most technically demanding piece. I integrated three payment providers — Paystack, Flutterwave, and Hubtel — to ensure customers could pay regardless of their bank or mobile money provider. All three required separate webhook handlers and different failure-recovery paths.

The REST API was built with PostgreSQL and Prisma ORM. Schema design was critical: orders, items, and payments had to maintain referential integrity even during partial failures. I used database transactions throughout the order creation flow to prevent ghost orders.
