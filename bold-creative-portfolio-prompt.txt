CASE STUDIES:

Case Study 01 — Dear Stranger — Making vulnerability feel safe
Role: UI/UX Designer — Class Project with Friends
The Story: My sister moved to the United States and even though she was surrounded by people every single day she could not find a way to make real connections. Social media is performance. Messaging apps feel like tasks. Everything moves too fast. I brought this idea to my team and we aligned on one question — what would it look like to build something where slowness was the whole point?
The Problem: There is a very specific kind of loneliness no existing platform addresses — the feeling of being somewhere new, surrounded by strangers, with no real way in. Every tool available was built for people who already have a social life.
My Design Contributions: As the UI/UX designer on the team I was responsible for the entire visual direction and user experience. Dark void background because the universe between people should feel infinite. Gold accents because against all that darkness gold feels rare and warm. Cinzel for labels because it has a timeless almost ancient quality. Cormorant Garamond for letters because it reads like handwriting. Each user gets a unique animated hub because no one should feel like just another profile. AI generated portraits so your presence feels like yours not a placeholder.
What Collaboration Looked Like: I led all design decisions while working closely with teammates on feature priorities and user flows. We tested the star map discovery experience together and iterated based on feedback before shipping.
Core Flow: Discover on the star map → Visit a hub → Write a letter → Wait for a reply
Outcome: Live at dearstranger.xyz with organic traffic from Google, Discord, and LinkedIn with zero marketing. 37 page views in the first week with real users actively creating hubs and writing letters.
Built With: React, Next.js, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Framer Motion, Supabase, OpenAI API, Vercel

Case Study 02 — DAS Sterile Training Center — From cluttered to clear
Role: UI/UX Designer and Frontend Developer — Freelance Client Project
The Story: A healthcare training company needed a website that would convince prospective students to enroll. Before designing anything I researched the industry thoroughly and studied every competitor site I could find.
The Problem: Every sterile processing school website I found had the same problems — poor color usage, no clear sections, everything crammed onto one page with no visual hierarchy. People making career decisions need to feel confident immediately. These sites were doing the opposite.
Research Findings: Competitor sites lacked visual hierarchy, used color inconsistently, had poor information architecture, and communicated no credibility or professionalism.
Design Decisions: Clear distinct sections so information is easy to find. Clean typography and generous spacing to communicate professionalism. Prominent application form with autosave. Testimonial carousel for social proof. FAQ accordion to answer common questions. Google Maps embed for local trust and discoverability.
Core Flow: Land → Learn about the program → Read testimonials → Apply
Outcome: Live at dascstraining.com actively serving real users. Client can manage content independently through WordPress. Delivered on time and handed off cleanly.
Built With: React, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, React Router, Web3Forms, WordPress, Vercel

Case Study 03 — Gobɛ Hemaa — Bringing a family business online
Role: Lead UI/UX Designer — Personal Project
The Story: My cousin in Ghana started a food business and wanted me to invest. I could not afford to invest money so I offered something better — I built her a platform. Gobɛ Hemaa means Queen of Food in Twi. Every design decision was made to honor that name.
The Problem: My cousin had no online presence. Existing food ordering platforms in Ghana either did not support local payment methods or felt completely disconnected from Ghanaian culture. She needed something that felt like hers.
Design Decisions: Warmth and color to reflect the culture and brand. Simple layout so ordering is fast and frictionless — cultural expression should never slow down the transaction. Designed for both success and failure states in the payment flow so no customer ever loses trust.
Core Flow: Browse menu → Add to cart → Create account → Checkout → Confirm order
Payment Challenge: Integrating Paystack, Flutterwave, and Hubtel to support Mobile Money and cash on delivery required designing checkout flows for each payment method's unique steps and failure scenarios.
Outcome: Live at gob3hemaa.vercel.app. A real product built for a real business, designed with cultural intention and shipped from scratch.
Built With: React, TypeScript, Node.js, Express.js, PostgreSQL, Prisma ORM, Neon, Paystack, Flutterwave, Hubtel, Vercel
